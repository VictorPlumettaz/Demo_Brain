# Pane Relief Software Ltd.

We make software for the flat glass industry — the plants that cut, temper, laminate and
assemble glass for buildings, furniture and vehicles. One main product, [[paneflow]], plus a
handful of satellites around it.

I wrote this page in my first weeks because the onboarding deck answered none of the questions
I actually had, like who decides what and which team owns which half of the product.

## The name

"Pane Relief" is a pun on "pain relief". The founder, Hartmut Lange, has been asked about it
for twenty-eight years and has never once apologised for it. A framed printout hangs in
reception: an email from a prospect in 2001 saying the name made them doubt we were a serious
supplier. They signed four months later and are still a customer.

## Customers

Around 200 glass processing plants across Europe: Germany, Poland, Italy, the Netherlands,
Czechia, Austria, Spain, the UK. Not the float lines that make raw glass — the processors who
buy that glass and turn it into windows, doors, facades, shower screens and furniture.

They range from family businesses with thirty people and one cutting table to groups running
four plants and three shifts. The small ones use maybe a third of [[paneflow]] and know that
third extremely well. The big ones use all of it and want more.

Named on this vault: [[glaswerk_nord_overview|Glaswerk Nord GmbH]], Bremen, currently onboarding.

development has. That gap is the reason half our meetings exist.
