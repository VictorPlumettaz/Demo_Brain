# Lena Mullion

Customer success at [[pane_relief_software|Pane Relief]] and owner of the
[[glaswerk_nord_overview|Glaswerk Nord GmbH]] relationship. Ten years as a production planner
in a glass plant before she joined, which is why she can talk to both sides without a translator.

## What she owns

- The Glaswerk Nord account: escalations, on-site training, the go-live plan
- Requirements from the customer, before they reach us as tickets
- The quarterly call where the plant says what actually hurts

## How to work with her

- She asks: "what does that mean for their Monday shift?" Have that answer ready. Not the
  cause — the consequence.
- Send the consequence, not the mechanism. "Yield drops about three points on coated batches
  until Friday" travels to the customer. "The remnant scoring changed" does not.
- If I need to know how a plant really works, she will put me on a call with their planner.
  Asked twice, got it the same week both times.
- She pushes back hard on our dates for the customer, then defends the agreed date to the
  customer just as hard. Both halves are the same job.
- Long mails from her are the customer's words pasted in. The last paragraph is hers. Read
  that one first.

## What I learned from her

- A plant does not stop the line for a hotfix. Anything needing a restart waits for the shift
  break, and the shift break does not move → [[howto_release_a_hotfix]].
- Their cutting tables run two shifts, Monday to Friday. That is why a nightly job at 03:00
  is fine and one at 05:00 is not.
- Half of what arrives as a bug is a plant working differently from the plant we imagined
  → [[support_ticket_patterns]].
- Vocabulary. Lite, build-up, remnant. I nodded through three calls understanding none of it,
  which is why [[glossary_glass_industry]] exists.
