# Dana Frames

Team lead of the PaneFlow Production team at [[pane_relief_software|Pane Relief Software]]
and my supervisor. Nine developers, one apprentice, and the roadmap for everything between
order entry and the cutting table.

## What she owns

- Priorities for the team. When two things are urgent she picks one, out loud, same day.
- The [[cutting_optimizer_rewrite_overview]]. She pushed for it and she carries it upstairs.
- Sign-off on anything that changes a number a customer sees.
- My apprenticeship plan, together with the training officer.

## How to work with her

- **Bring a decision, not a problem.** "X is broken, I would do A, costs a day" gets an
  answer in a minute. "X is broken, what now?" gets a meeting, and she does not want a meeting.
- **Half a page beats a call.** She reads short written proposals. She does not read three pages.
- The meetings she books are 15 minutes and she ends them at 15 minutes, mid-sentence if needed.
- She decides fast and reverses herself in public when she was wrong. Nobody keeps score,
  including her. So an old decision is not settled if the facts moved — but say that out loud.
- Bad news early is fine. Bad news late is the only thing I have seen actually annoy her.
- "Fine, do it your way" is not sarcasm. She means it, and it does not come back at me later.

## What I learned from her

- A decision made on Monday and corrected on Thursday beats a perfect decision made in three
  weeks. After that it stops being speed and turns into debt.
- Estimate in "what could go wrong", not in "how long if nothing does".
- Write down *why*, not *what*. Git already knows what → [[cutting_optimizer_rewrite_decisions]].
