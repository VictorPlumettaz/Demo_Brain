## Notes

Marco checks a cutting pattern by looking at the **trim edge first**, not at the big pieces in the
middle. If the trim along the outside of the sheet is wrong, everything inside it has been arranged
around a mistake and the nice-looking parts in the middle mean nothing. I would have started in the
middle, because that is where the interesting shapes are.

Same idea as checking the boundary case before the happy path. It just took a glass example for it to
land → [[glossary_glass_industry]]
