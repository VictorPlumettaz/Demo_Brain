# 2026-08-17 — Monday

## What happened

I asked [[marco_bevel]] about the yield defect. He found it in about ten minutes. Remnant area is
counted twice. I had been on it since Tuesday.

## How he found it

He asked two questions before opening any code.

**Does it go wrong on every batch, or only some?** Only some — three of [[priya_tempered]]'s
batches, none of mine.

**What do the failing ones have in common?** I did not know, so we looked. All three failing batches
produce remnants above the reuse threshold. A remnant is a leftover piece of a stock sheet that is
still big enough to put back into stock; the default threshold at this plant is 300 mm on the short
edge. My fixture set is forty rectangles on standard jumbo sheets. Every offcut it produces is under
300 mm, so nothing ever goes back to stock, so the bug cannot fire.

Then the code, which took four minutes. `CommitPattern` adds the whole cut area of the sheet to the
used-area total. `RegisterRemnant` then adds the remnant area to the same total. So a remnant counts
once as glass we consumed and once as glass we recovered. On a batch with a lot of odd sizes that is
enough to push the reported yield past 100%.

## Done

- Root cause found: remnant area added in both `CommitPattern` and `RegisterRemnant` → [[cutting_optimizer_rewrite_decisions]]
- Verified against all three of Priya's batches and against my fixture set
- Wrote the reproduction condition into the ticket: the defect needs at least one remnant above the
  plant reuse threshold. Without that, the numbers are correct.
